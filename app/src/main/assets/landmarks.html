Key Landmarks in Gezawa Town

  • Gezawa Central Market

    The largest open-air marketplace in the area, offering grains, produce, textiles, and local crafts every market day.

  • Emir’s Palace

    The traditional residence of the Emir of Gezawa, reflecting Hausa architectural styles and local heritage.

  • Old Grain Silos

    Historic colonial-era silos that once stored surplus crops; now a reminder of the region’s agricultural legacy.

  • Local Handicraft Centre

    Workshops and shops where artisans produce leather goods, woven mats, pottery, and dyed textiles.

  • Almajiri Schools

    Informal Quranic schools where children study under local scholars, reflecting the town’s religious and educational traditions.
